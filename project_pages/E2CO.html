Abstract


Existing gaze-based smartphone interaction methods generally need the auxiliary of hand motions to realize control, and most of them can achieve only simple control operations, which are far from practical smartphone control. To this end, we propose a novel eye-gaze-based smartphone interaction method named Event-driven Eye-Gaze-Controlled Operation (E²GCO), which can realize comprehensive smartphone control operations just using human eyes and gazes. We designed four pairs of eye-gaze-based interaction actions that are easy to implement and distinguish, based on which E²GCO can control all kinds of smartphone pages. Before the interaction, an anti-jitter strategy-based gaze estimation method is exploited to solve the jitter problem of human eye fixation and detect both accurate and stable human gaze locations on smartphone screens, even when the users are moving. The interaction can be executed more accurately based on the stable and accurate gaze locations. In addition, the event camera technology is introduced in E²GCO to significantly decrease its power consumption, making E²GCO more power-efficient to deploy on smartphones. We have implemented the prototype of E²GCO on different brands of smartphones and conducted a comprehensive user study to validate its efficacy. The user study demonstrate E²GCO's superior smartphone control capabilities across various scenarios.

Contributions

  1. We propose a novel method that relies solely on eye-tracking information, significantly changing how users interact with smartphones.

  2. We introduce an innovative approach for calculating user gaze positions, offering a fresh perspective on attention measurement and utilization.

  3. We incorporate event camera technology into our system, substantially enhancing device battery life and enabling extended usage in diverse scenarios.
